🔑 1. Primary Key:
                  • A Primary Key is a unique identifier for each record (row) in a table.
                  • It makes sure no two rows are the same and every row can be uniquely found.

📘 Rules:
          • It must be unique (no duplicates)
          • It cannot be NULL (must have a value)

📊 Example:
---------------------------------------------------
|   StudentID     |     name    |       Age       |
---------------------------------------------------
|       1         |     John    |       20        |
---------------------------------------------------
|       2         |     Mary    |       22        |
---------------------------------------------------

👉 Here, StudentID is the Primary Key — each student has a unique ID.

✅ Think of it like: Your Aadhar number or Roll number — unique to you.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

🔐 2. Composite Key:

A Composite Key is a combination of two or more columns that together make a unique identifier for each record.

📊 Example:
---------------------------------------------
|   StudentID	  | CourseID    |   Grade     |
---------------------------------------------
|       1       |     101     |     A       |
---------------------------------------------
|       1       |     102     |     B       |
---------------------------------------------
|       2       |     101     |     A       |
---------------------------------------------

👉 Here, neither StudentID nor CourseID alone is unique —
   but together (StudentID + CourseID) form a Composite Key.

✅ Think of it like: A combination of two pieces of information (e.g., “student + course”) that makes a record unique.


---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

🔗 3. Foreign Key:

• A Foreign Key is used to link two tables together.
• It is a column that refers to the Primary Key of another table.

📊 Example:

Students Table:
---------------------------------
|   StudentID	  |     Name      |
---------------------------------
|     1         |     John      |
---------------------------------
|     2         |     Mary      |
---------------------------------

Enrollments Table:
-----------------------------------------------
|   EnrollmentID  |	StudentID   |   Course    |
-----------------------------------------------
|       101       |   1         |   Math      |
-----------------------------------------------
|       102       |   2         |   Science   |
-----------------------------------------------

👉 Here, StudentID in the Enrollments table is a Foreign Key —
   it points to StudentID in the Students table.

✅ Think of it like: A reference or link — it connects data between tables.
