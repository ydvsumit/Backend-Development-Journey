🔥 What is mean of Remove transitive dependencies ?
💡 Meaning: 
          transitive dependency means one column depends on another non-key column, instead of depending directly on the primary key.

👉 So when we say “remove transitive dependencies”, it means:
    • Make sure that non-key columns depend only on the primary key, not on other non-key columns.


📌 Example (Before removing transitive dependency):
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
|   StudentID | StudentName   |   DepartmentID  |   DepartmentName    |
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
|       1     |   John        |       D1        |   Computer Science  |
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
|       2     |   Mary        |       D2        |   Electrical        |
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
|       3     |   Sam         |       D1        |   Computer Science  |
-----------------------------------------------------------------------

👉 Primary key: StudentID

Now check dependencies:
                      • StudentID → DepartmentID ✅ (good — depends on key)
                      • But also, DepartmentID → DepartmentName ❌ (depends on another non-key column)

That second one is a transitive dependency —
                                            • DepartmentName depends indirectly on StudentID through DepartmentID.


📌 Fix (After removing transitive dependency):

We split the table into two:

👉Students Table:
-----------------------------------------------------
|   StudentID   |   StudentName   |   DepartmentID  |
-----------------------------------------------------
|       1       |       John      |       D1        |
-----------------------------------------------------
|       2       |       Mary      |       D2        |
-----------------------------------------------------
|       3       |       Sam       |       D1        |
-----------------------------------------------------


👉Departments Table:
-----------------------------------------
| DepartmentID    |   DepartmentName    |
-----------------------------------------
|     D1          |   Computer Science  |
-----------------------------------------
|     D2          |   Electrical        |
-----------------------------------------

Now:
    • In Students, all columns depend only on StudentID.
    • In Departments, DepartmentName depends only on DepartmentID.

✅ No transitive dependency now.


🧠 In short:
            • A transitive dependency = column depends on another non-key column.
            • To remove it, separate the table into smaller tables so that each column depends only on the primary key.


✅ Simple Example Analogy:
Imagine:
        • Your Roll Number identifies you.
        • But your Teacher Name depends on your Subject, not on your Roll Number directly.
          → That’s a transitive dependency.
          So, we move “Subject → Teacher” info to another table.
